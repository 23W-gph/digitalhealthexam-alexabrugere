1. False. Smoking is the leading cause of disease and disability and harms nearly every organ of the body.

2. False. Cigarette smoking, not obesity, is the leading cause of preventable disease, disability, and death in the United States.

3. True. The tobacco industry spends billions of dollars each year on marketing cigarettes.

4. True. Smoking costs the United States hundreds of billions of dollars each year.

5. False. States do not spend much of the money they get from tobacco taxes and lawsuits to prevent smoking and help smokers quit. CDC recommends that states spend 12% of those funds on tobacco control.

6. False. In 2021, 11.5% of U.S. adults (an estimated 28.3 million people) currently smoked cigarettes: 13.1% of men, 10.1% of women.

7. False. In 2023, of those youth who ever tried e-cigarettes, approximately half reported currently using them, indicating that many youths who try e-cigarettes remain e-cigarette users.

8. False. Many adult cigarette smokers want to quit smoking.
